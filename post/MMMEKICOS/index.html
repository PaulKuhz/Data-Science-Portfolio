Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) and Match Market Testing (MMT): FY24 Analysis for S&P 500 Cosmetique Company

March 12, 2024

This project marked my debut as a data science intern at Ekimetrics in New York. Our team of three analysts meticulously examined the entire US operations of an S&P 500 cosmetics company. We focused on the period from January 2023 to December 2023, analyzing data across three key channels: Brand.com, Retailer.com, and Brick & Mortar (B&M). Throughout the project, we processed a substantial 10.5 gigabytes of data. For confidentiality reasons, all figures have been slightly altered.

Data Processing

With a total Sales value of $703M, the company experienced a 2% increase in FY24 H1 compared to FY23 H1, while the contribution mix remained consistent with 1% of sales shifting from B&M to Retailer.com. Peaks in sales were seen during Cyber Monday and the End of Summer Sales, while the highest sales were reached by Treatment (50%), Makeup (41%), and Fragrance (6%) products.

In FY24H1, Web Traffic remained stable compared to FY23H1, indicating consistent online engagement. There was a 19% increase in Emails delivered in FY24H1 compared to FY23H1, but revenue remained stable with a slight year-over-year decline of 5%. The total spend on emails was $208,000. The number of Brick-and-Mortar Stores continued to grow steadily post-COVID and this trend persisted into FY24. Sales for Sets and PWP (Purchase With Purchase) remained consistent between FY23H1 and FY24H1. In FY24H1, the cost of Goods Sold was $32.2 million for sets, $9.9 million for PWP, and $3.8 million for samples. GWP (Gift With Purchase) costs increased by 26% in FY24H1. Samples activity saw a decline of 17%, primarily in the Retailer.com channel. COOP costs decreased by 15%, down from 85% in FY23H1.

Example Slide for Validation created with Power BI

The company significantly increased its Meta Spend by 44%, focusing heavily on awareness (up by 216%) and consideration (up by 20%) campaigns. Snapchat Spend and TikTok Spend increased by 38%, with significant rises in awareness investment. Pinterest Spend, however, decreased by 29%. Brand Search investments decreased by 24%. OLV (Online Video) spend saw a substantial increase of 300%. Paid Influencer investments surged by 176%, resulting in a 22% increase in EMV (Earned Media Value). Affiliate clicks decreased by 18%, alongside a 9% reduction in spending. Brand.com PWP sales rose by 42% between FY23H1 and FY24H1, demonstrating strong promotional performance, especially from July to October. Brand.com GWP costs increased by 48%, while samples activity on the brand.com platform declined by 64%.

Overall, FY24H1 highlighted stable sales figures but pointed to increased marketing expenditures, particularly in digital and social media channels. The results showed mixed outcomes in terms of efficiency and return on investment, underscoring the need for ongoing optimization of marketing strategies.

Methodology and Modeling

Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM): Multi-stage MMM employs a top-down measurement approach to evaluate the incremental impact of traditional and digital media on brand metrics. Utilizing multi-variate regression modeling, it isolates the true incremental sell-thru impact of A&P tactics. The key outputs of this technique are the incremental ROI and the percentage contribution.

Match Market Testing (MMT): Near-time A/B testing is utilized for the granular optimization of specific tactics, with the key outputs being incremental ROI and percentage lift.

Key Findings

1. Retail Sales Increase: In FY24 H1, retail sales increased by $14M (+2%) compared to FY23 H1. This growth was driven by increased investment in CE Media, expanded Ulta distribution, and effective promotional strategies.

2. Media Impact: Media-driven sales decreased by $11.8M due to a $3.2M reduction in spend. Despite this, total media ROI remained stable at 131%. Ecomm media maintained profitability with an ROI of 191%, although rising costs resulted in a $5M reduction in incremental sales. Coop media sustained a profitable ROI of 104%, highlighting opportunities for optimizing the media mix to enhance efficiency further.

Model Output displayed in Layer Curves

3. Strategic Media Changes: Strategic Media Changes: Increased investment in CE Media by $2M resulted in a $7M sales increase, maintaining a flat ROI of 122% due to market saturation. OLV spend decreased by $1.9M, with a shift to Google Programmatic OLV achieving a boosted ROI of 145%. Search strategy expanded into Non-Brand and Brand PMAX, both profitable but less efficient compared to PLA.

4. Challenges and Opportunities: Rising costs saw Brand Search's CPM increase by 256%, impacting ROI which dropped to 85%. A strategic shift away from efficient social tactics in Coop Media led to a significant $13.8M sales decline. Promotions, specifically Sets and GWPs, contributed 15.8% to overall sales. Organic TikTok efforts amplified paid TikTok impact by 1.5x, highlighting strong synergy and growth potential.

5. Efficiency Insights: CE Media proves effective across the funnel with a 122% ROI, although Facebook and IG show reduced profitability. Social Media performs strongly, especially in conversion, with promising initial results from TikTok testing. Affiliate Marketing maintains stable incremental profit ROI, showing increased efficiency in H2.

6. Media Cost Impact: The impact of media costs was significant. Ecomm media, which accounted for 57% of the spend, experienced a 43% increase in CPM, leading to a 22pp decline in ROI. Conversely, Snapchat's better performance helped offset the cost impacts.

7. Outlook: Future directions include continued testing on TikTok and further investment and testing in RMN tactics, as Ulta RMN has shown significant returns.

Key Takeaways: The Company maintained a stable and profitable media ROI despite rising costs and spending shifts. Strategic media mix changes and effective promotional tactics drove sales growth. Future efficiency and effectiveness can be enhanced through optimization and further testing in TikTok, PMAX campaigns, and RMN tactics.

Optimization

A FY25 H1 plan optimized based on measured ROIs can yield up to an additional +$8.8M in media-driven incremental retail sales (+$3.6M in profit) by increasing investments in the efficient Consideration bucket. There is an opportunity to further increase incremental retail sales by +$8.8M vs. the current plan by reinvesting the planned Awareness budget in saturated tactics into the efficient Consideration bucket.
