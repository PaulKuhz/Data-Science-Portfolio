Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM): FY2024 Analysis of an S&P 500 Fashion Company

June 23, 2024

As a data science intern at Ekimetrics, I collaborated with three analysts from the company's London office to conduct an in-depth analysis of the performance of a leading S&P 500 company in the UK fashion industry. Our analysis focused on evaluating both short-term and long-term media return on investment, assessing the brand halo impact of luxury campaigns across various channels, and measuring the media memory impact on current transactions. We also reported the contributions of different media channels by campaign type, such as lifestyle and commerce, and evaluated the impact of events, sponsorships, and c elebrity endorsements on both short-term and long-term business performance. It is important to note that, due to data privacy concerns, all figures in the analysis have been slightly modified.

Data Set

The sales data includes discount percentages and unit prices across different business units: RLS (Retail), RLE (E-commerce), and PRO (Outlet). The discount percentage for RLS was 10.0% in FY22, 7.7% in FY23, and 10.2% in FY24. The unit price for RLS increased from £196 in FY22 to £226 in FY23 and £256 in FY24. Similar trends are observed in other business units.

CRM data includes emails and SMS data, with specific figures for each business unit. For instance, in FY24, the number of non-promotional emails sent by the PRO unit increased to 19.5 million from 17.8 million in FY23, while promotional emails sent rose to 24.5 million from 15.4 million. Spending on CRM activities also varied, with PRO spending £0.5 million in FY24, up from £0.4 million in FY23.

Transaction data is meticulously categorized into matched and unmatched transactions, detailed by store codes and transaction types such as purchases and returns. Key business performance drivers are identified, including baseline drivers like COVID-19 impacts, store counts, retail trends, and competitor media spend. Marketing drivers encompass product launches, sponsorships, in-store marketing, wholesale media activation, own media activation, public relations activities, promotions, and CRM efforts.

Methodology and Modeling

A Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) followed a step-by-step approach to measure the incrementality of marketing activities, incorporating external factors and internal business patterns. Key stages involved adding seasonal patterns, media inputs (TV GRPs, OOH), and product launches, which improved the model's predictive ability, achieving a high R² of 98%.

Key business drivers were identified and categorized into baseline and marketing drivers. Baseline drivers included factors such as COVID-19 impact, mobility (using Google data on public location visits), seasonality (events like Christmas, Black Friday, and Bank Holidays), retail trends (data from the Office for National Statistics), door count (number of open stores), market conditions (Google search volume for competitors), competition (competitor media spend), and pricing (MSRP and outlet pricing).

Luxury Layer Curve

Marketing drivers included product launches (e.g., RLX), Wimbledon sponsorship (reach and PR impact), in-store marketing (events and window displays), wholesale media (partnerships with ASOS and Farfetch), own media (campaigns by Assembly/Publicis/Silverbean), PR activities (celebrity endorsements, fashion shows), promotions (promotional periods and markdowns), and CRM efforts (emails, SMS campaigns).

ROI and media impact were analyzed, with gross revenue ROIs calculated by multiplying weekly transactions by average transaction value (ATV). Total ROI was determined by comparing incremental demand revenue to investment, while direct ROI measured revenue generated directly from marketing activities and indirect ROI measured revenue influenced by brand metrics. The multiplier effect showed that for every $1 of direct revenue, media drives an additional $0.07 by influencing brand KPIs.

Promotions and CRM impacts were significant. Promotions were captured by discount percentage and analyzed for planning purposes. CRM effectiveness was measured by engagement metrics such as open and click rates. Emails were categorized into promotional, non-promotional, and private sale emails.

Performance insights revealed that media had an average ROI of 2.8:1 in the UK, higher than the luxury fashion average of 2.5:1. Social, OOH, and print media were identified as efficient upper funnel drivers, while digital, mobile, and display media were efficient lower funnel drivers. Promotions significantly impacted consideration, while media and PR had substantial effects on wholesale channels.

Channel performance was also detailed, showing that baseline drivers (natural demand) constituted a significant portion of sales across channels. Direct-to-consumer (DTC) channels saw higher impacts from media and CRM activities, while wholesale channels benefited from combined impacts of the company's own and retailer media efforts. CRM contributed 9% of the 2023 gross revenue, primarily from emails.

In summary, the presentation highlighted strong natural demand with baseline drivers contributing to 60% or more of sales across channels. Media and CRM were crucial for driving additional demand, especially in RLE. Promotions had a significant incremental contribution across channels, emphasizing the need for strategic planning and optimization in future marketing efforts.

Optimization

A highly accurate predictive model with an R² of 98% was developed, allowing effective media budget allocation and optimization of marketing activities to maximize ROI and revenue. The project workshop's final presentation detailed the use of Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) to assess the incremental impact of marketing efforts, starting with baseline estimations that included seasonal patterns and media inputs like TV GRPs and OOH advertising.

Optimization Summary by Channel

The final model integrated online display advertising interactions with other channels, demonstrating strong predictive accuracy essential for strategic decision-making. This resulted in an additional +$2.7M in incremental Revenue and +7% in ROI.

Scenario testing explored the impact of different strategies, media spends, consumer behavior changes, and competitive actions. MMM helped identify the most effective channels and tactics, enabling the client to adapt its marketing approach to varying conditions and maintain competitiveness in the UK market.
